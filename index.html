Robot Arena

A Unity-based battle sandbox where you program robot behavior with Scratch-like visual blocks, tune loadouts, and watch your strategy fight in a deterministic physics arena.

Unity 2022.3 LTS Visual Block Editor Deterministic Physics

Core Systems

Robot Arena is built on four major pillars that work together to create the battle experience.

🧩

Visual Block Programming

Drag-and-drop Scratch-style blocks from a palette into a workspace. Blocks snap together vertically and nest inside control structures. Supports variables, custom subroutines, event-triggered behaviors, and full JSON import/export.

🔧

Accessory Loadout System

Equip mobility parts, weapons, armor, sensors, and special modules. Each accessory consumes power from a limited budget, modifies robot stats, can unlock exclusive blocks, and may grant active abilities like radial knockback or enemy slowdown.

⚙️

Deterministic Simulation

Matches run on a fixed tick clock with a custom physics world. Every run with the same programs produces the same outcome. Supports projectile ricochet, knockback, fall-off-map detection, and capture zone scoring.

📡

Sensor & Event System

Robots can sense the environment around them — obstacles ahead, map edges, enemy proximity, incoming projectiles, and contact with walls or opponents. Event blocks constantly monitor conditions and can interrupt normal execution when triggered.

Match Rules & Win Conditions

Every round follows a strict simulation loop with multiple ways to win or lose. Understanding these rules is essential for building effective strategies.

Round Lifecycle

1
Loadout Validation
The game checks that required accessories are equipped and the power budget is not exceeded before allowing the round to start.
2
World Bake
Scene geometry — ground, walls, obstacles — is converted into a simplified collision world for deterministic simulation. Both robots are placed at their start positions with full health.
3
Active Round
The tick clock starts. Player and enemy programs execute concurrently. Projectiles fly, sensors poll, events fire, health bars drain. The round continues until a terminal condition is met.
4
Resolution
When the round ends, a winner is determined by the rules below. A 2-second death/draw presentation plays, then the result popup is shown.

How to Win (or Lose)

WIN — Deplete the Enemy

Each robot has a health pool. Bullet hits deal damage. When the enemy's health reaches zero, you win immediately. Accessories can modify both max health and damage per hit, so loadout choices directly affect time-to-kill.

WIN — Knock the Enemy Off the Map

The arena is a raised platform. If a robot falls below a threshold distance from the platform surface, it is instantly eliminated regardless of remaining health. Knockback from projectiles, ricochets, and special abilities can push robots over the edge.

WIN — Capture Zone Control

Some arena layouts include a capture point near the center. If a robot controls the zone when time expires, it wins — even if both robots are still alive. The capture zone can change hands during the round depending on robot presence.

LOSE — Your Robot is Depleted

The mirror of the above: if your health hits zero (from bullets or falling), the enemy wins. Damage from your own ricocheting bullets can hurt you too.

DRAW — Mutual Destruction

If both robots are depleted in the same tick (e.g., simultaneous knockback off the map, or a final exchange of fire), the round ends in a draw. No winner is declared.

What Happens When Time Runs Out?

Each round has a configurable time limit (default 30 seconds, adjustable via the timer button). When the clock expires without a knockout:

  • If one robot controls the capture zone, they win outright.
  • Otherwise, the robot that took less total damage during the round wins — this is a "judge's decision" based on combat effectiveness.
  • If damage taken is equal, the round is a draw.

Program Completion

Both player and enemy programs run simultaneously. If both programs finish executing their main chain (i.e., reach the end of their block sequence without being in a loop) before the timer expires and before either robot is destroyed, the round stops and the winner is resolved by the same damage-comparison rule used for timeouts.

Damage Mechanics

Bullets deal a fixed base damage per hit. Each robot's damage output can be modified by weapon accessories (via a damage multiplier stat). Bullets ricochet off walls — a single shot can potentially hit multiple times. Each ricochet reduces remaining bullet lifetime, so infinite bounces are not possible. Damage is disabled before the round starts and after it ends, so there's no pre-match or post-match griefing.

Accessory System

Accessories are the customization backbone of your robot. Each one occupies a specific equipment slot, consumes a portion of your power budget, and can dramatically alter your robot's combat performance. The ACCESSORIES tab provides a visual preview of equipped parts and shows which blocks each accessory unlocks.

Equipment Slots & Categories

🛞

Mobility — Wheels & Tracks

Determines how fast your robot moves and turns. Different mobility options apply duration multipliers to movement and rotation actions. Faster mobility means you can cover more ground per action block, but high-performance parts cost more power. Mobility is a single-select slot — you can only equip one at a time.

🔫

Weapon — Cannons & Lasers

Controls your offensive output. Weapons can modify muzzle velocity (how fast projectiles travel), fire rate (time between shots), and damage multiplier (how hard each hit lands). Some weapons also define a maximum effective range with damage falloff — shots beyond optimal distance deal reduced damage. Equipping a weapon also unlocks the Fire block in the programming palette.

🛡️

Defense — Armor, Shields, Jetpacks

Armor adds a flat bonus to your robot's maximum health pool and may provide secondary benefits like increased sensor range. Shields and jetpacks occupy this same single-select slot and provide different defensive or mobility trade-offs. The defense slot is crucial for survivability — a robot with no armor is fragile.

📡

Sensor — Radar

Extends the range at which your robot can detect enemies and obstacles. A longer sensor range means your robot can "see" threats from farther away, which is critical for environment-aware strategies that use visibility checks. Sensor range multiplies the base detection distance of all sensing blocks.

⚡

Special — Bouncer & Freezer

Active abilities that can turn the tide of battle. These occupy the Special slot and each has its own cooldown timer.

Special Abilities in Detail

💥

Bouncer

A radial knockback burst centered on your robot. When activated, it applies a physics impulse to targets within a configurable radius. Extremely effective for pushing enemies off the map edge or breaking line-of-sight. Has a cooldown between uses, and some Bouncer variants can only fire once per round.

❄️

Freezer

Temporarily multiplies the target enemy's movement and firing speed by a fraction, effectively slowing them down for a configurable duration. Must be activated within range of the enemy robot. The freeze effect wears off after its duration expires, but can be re-applied once the cooldown resets. Does not deal direct damage.

💡 Weapon Range & Linear Falloff: Every weapon accessory can define a max range and a linear damage falloff curve. Within the falloff start distance, bullets deal 100% of their base damage. Beyond that point, damage decreases linearly with distance traveled — the farther the shot, the less it hurts. At max range, damage bottoms out at a configurable minimum multiplier (e.g. 20%). A weapon with maxRange = 0 has no range limit — its bullets never expire from distance and deal full damage at any range. This system rewards closing the gap and punishes long-range sniping unless the weapon is built for it. Check your weapon's stats in the ACCESSORIES tab to understand its effective engagement envelope.

Power Budget

Every accessory has a power consumption value. Both you and the enemy have a power ceiling — if your equipped loadout exceeds it, you cannot start the round. This forces meaningful trade-offs: you can't equip the best of everything. The current power usage is displayed next to the global controls and updates in real time as you change equipment.

Stat Bonuses

Accessories modify your robot's performance through cumulative stat bonuses. Multiple equipped parts can stack their effects. The key stats affected are: movement speed, rotation speed, maximum health, damage dealt per hit, projectile muzzle velocity, fire rate, and sensor detection range. Some accessories also unlock blocks that would otherwise be hidden in the palette — you literally cannot use certain abilities without the right equipment.

Strategy & Competition

Programs are stored and shared as JSON files. You can export your design, import opponent strategies, or hand-author programs. A strategy competition guide is included in the project repository.
